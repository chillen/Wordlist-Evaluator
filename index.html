Read the stimulus word on the left, then read the related list on the right. Each word should be related to the stimulus.

Beside each word is 5 buttons representing issues you may find with that word. Click each issue you feel is relevant, then press "Next". Alternatively, you can use the arrow keys to navigate words and press 1-6 to toggle issues. Spacebar brings you to the next trial.

The potential issues are:

  • Unrelated (1): The word has no apparent relation to the stimulus.
  • Synonymous (2): The word is synonymous with the stimulus.
  • Obvious (3): The word has a very obvious relation (eg. compound word) and fails to stimulate new ideas.
  • Dull (4): The word is boring, archaic, or meaningless, and fails to stimulate new ideas.
  • Repetative (5): This word, while possibly fine on its own, is too closely related to one or more other words in the list.
  • Other (6): You feel the word is out of place, but does not fit into any of these categories.
